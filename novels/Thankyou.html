A thank you from 10 years ago

Dear Professor Arbo Valdma:

I want to say thank you for things that happened ten years ago. They aren’t something very special actually.

At that time, I was a very young kid, and you were teaching me piano for a “master course” (I’m unsure it is the correct words for “大师班”, anyway I was never a formal student of yours), in Guangzhou, China; and you did two things to me. One is told me to “treat piano as my friend”, and the second is that you took a photo of my hand.

Up to now, I’m still not, and never will be a pianist; and what’s worse: I’m never going to touch piano ever, which is a long story. Instead, now I occasionally compose sort of songs to entertain myself and to “discover emotions”. But they’re not my reason to thank. You succeeded in making a special impression on me(no one has ever taken photos of my hand), giving me one of many of valuable memories to recall and to rethink. Memory is very important to me, putting into my thinking that, if you cannot recall, you acts like souless dolls. There should be more words to write for feelings and thoughts, even just for that unseen photo. But my brain is still too weak and small.

Hope I haven’t disturbed your life,

Guanda Yuan 袁冠达
